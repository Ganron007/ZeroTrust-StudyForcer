## Why this file exists

In a previous conversation I asked what it would actually take to turn the Study Planner into a **$100M ARR SaaS**. The answer was: 10 years, ~$150M of someone else's money, ~250 employees, and a 1–2% chance of success even on perfect execution. I declined. Acting pays better per delusion.

This file is the consolation prize — a working **product-discovery kit** in case parallel-universe-me ever shows up, or anyone else stumbles in here wanting to take a swing at it.

Three docs in one:

1. **Positioning canvas** — pick the wedge, write it down, stop chasing every shiny vertical.
2. **Buyer-interview script** — what to actually say to 60–100 strangers before writing a line of new code.
3. **90-day discovery plan** — a calendar, not a vibe.

**This is not a business plan.** It is a "risk-removal kit." The fastest way to lose two years and $300k of savings is to skip Part 2 and start coding. The fastest way to find out you should pursue acting full-time is to actually run Part 3.

## Part 1: Positioning canvas

Fill this out before anything else. If you can't, you don't have a product — you have a feature.

### Template

| Field | Your one-line answer |
|---|---|
| **Who is the buyer?** (the person who signs the PO, not the end user) | |
| **What is their #1 painful job-to-be-done?** | |
| **What do they use today instead?** | |
| **Why does that suck for them?** | |
| **What's the *one* thing we do that's 10× better?** | |
| **What's our wedge feature?** (the first thing they'd buy us for) | |
| **What's the trojan-horse expansion?** (what we sell them next) | |
| **What's the smallest ICP that gives us $1M ARR?** | |
| **Why us, why now, why not the incumbents?** | |

### Pre-filled draft for the "compliance / cert-tracking" wedge

Just to show what filling it out looks like — not a commitment, a starting point:

| Field | Draft answer |
|---|---|
| Buyer | Director of Compliance / VP People at 200–5,000-employee regulated companies (fintech, healthcare, SaaS handling PII). |
| Painful JTBD | Pass the annual SOC2 / ISO27001 / HIPAA audit; prove every employee completed required certs on time. |
| Status quo | Cornerstone or Workday Learning + Excel + Slack reminders. Manual reporting eats two weeks per quarter. |
| Why it sucks | LMS-centric tools track *courses*; auditors want *certifications + recurrence + proof*. Cert prep happens off-platform. |
| 10× better thing | Cert prep + tracking in one place; audit-ready reports in one click. |
| Wedge feature | Compliance dashboard + automated cert-renewal reminders. |
| Expansion | Per-seat cert prep content (CISSP, CISA, AWS, Azure, etc.) + manager dashboards. |
| Smallest $1M ARR ICP | 100 mid-market companies × $10k ACV, or 50 × $20k. |
| Why us | Built planner-first → learners actually use it. Incumbents nail tracking but not learning. |

If filling this in for real takes more than two afternoons, **you don't know your buyer yet — go interview people.**

## Part 2: Buyer-interview script

Run 60–100 of these before you trust any answer in Part 1. 30 minutes each, on Zoom, recorded with consent.

### Rules

1. **Never pitch.** The moment you describe your product, they start being polite and the data is worthless.
2. **Ask about the past, not the future.** "What did you do last quarter to prepare for the audit?" beats "Would you use a tool that…"
3. **Follow the pain.** If they say something hurt, ask "How did you handle that?" three more times.
4. **Get specifics, never generalities.** "How many hours?" "How many people?" "What tool exactly?"
5. **Shut up.** Talk less than 30% of the call. Their silence is where the real answer lives.

### The script

**Intro (2 min)** — "I'm researching how teams handle [problem area]. Not selling anything. I'd love to learn how you do this today. Mind if I record so I'm not scribbling?"

**Their world (5 min)**
- Walk me through your role and team.
- Who do you report to? Who reports to you?
- How is your team measured?

**The job (10 min)**
- Tell me about the last time you had to [the thing your product addresses].
- Walk me through it day by day. What did you do first? Then?
- Who else was involved?
- What tools did you open?
- What was the hardest part?
- What did you do when [the hard part] hit?

**The pain (8 min)**
- What's the single thing about this you'd change if you could?
- What have you tried that didn't work? Why didn't it work?
- If a magic wand fixed one part of this, which part? Why that part?
- What does this cost you — in time, money, or sanity?

**Budget & buying (3 min)** — *only if rapport is good*
- Do you have a budget line for [this problem area]?
- Who would sign off on a new tool here?
- What's the threshold below which you can just expense it vs. above which it becomes Procurement Theater™?

**Wrap (2 min)**
- Is there anyone else I should talk to?
- Can I come back to you in 4–6 weeks with what I've learned?

### After every call (within 1 hour)

Single doc, one row per interview:

| Date | Name | Role / Co. | Top 3 pains (in *their* words) | Status-quo tools | Cost of pain ($/hrs) | Quotable lines | Follow-up? |

The quotable lines are gold. They become your landing-page headlines later.

### Red flags that mean the wedge is wrong

- Everyone says "interesting" but no one says "where do I sign up."
- Pain is real but small ($5k/yr-worth, not $50k).
- Pain belongs to someone with no budget authority.
- Status-quo tool actually works fine and you just didn't know it.

If you hit any of those after 20 interviews, **change the wedge.** Do not push through.

## Part 3: 90-day discovery plan

A calendar, not a vibe.

### Days 1–14: Setup

- [ ] Write the positioning canvas (Part 1) — draft only; will rewrite at day 60 and day 90.
- [ ] Build interview pipeline: 200 LinkedIn outreaches at 30% reply rate → 60 calls booked.
- [ ] Set up Calendly + Zoom + recording + a single Notion/Linear DB for notes.
- [ ] Write the interview script (use Part 2 as starting point).
- [ ] Pick **one** wedge to interview against. One. Not two.

**Done-when:** 15+ interviews booked on the calendar.

### Days 15–45: Interview blitz

- [ ] Conduct 30–40 interviews. Yes, 30. Two per workday.
- [ ] Tag every quotable pain point with the JTBD it maps to.
- [ ] Weekly: count interviews that ended with a yes to follow-up. Below 50% = wedge is wrong.
- [ ] At day 30, write a one-page memo: "What I now believe vs. what I believed at day 0."

**Done-when:** you can predict what the next interview will say with ~70% accuracy. That's the sign the market is legible.

### Days 46–75: Wedge refinement + concierge MVP

- [ ] Rewrite the positioning canvas with what you actually learned.
- [ ] Pick 3 "warmest" interviewees and offer them a **manual concierge service** — *you* do the job by hand, $0, in exchange for weekly feedback.
- [ ] Do the job manually. Spreadsheets, scripts, whatever. **Do not build product yet.**
- [ ] Track: how long does it take to deliver the value? What would they pay if it were automated?

**Done-when:** 3 people have said *unprompted* "I would pay for this if it were a product."

### Days 76–90: Decision

Write a 2-page memo with three numbers:

1. **Estimated ACV** based on what 3 concierge customers said they'd pay.
2. **Estimated ICP size** in addressable companies/users (not TAM hand-wave — count).
3. **Estimated build cost** from concierge → MVP (in months and dollars, conservative 2×).

Then make one of four decisions:

| Decision | Trigger |
|---|---|
| **Go (raise / commit)** | All 3 numbers support a path to $10M+ ARR within 4 years. |
| **Lifestyle / bootstrap** | Real pain but ACV × ICP caps at $3–5M ARR. Worth doing for income, not for a $100M outcome. |
| **Pivot wedge** | Numbers don't work but interviews surfaced a *different* market that does. Restart the 90 days against that wedge. |
| **Kill it** | No real pain, no buyer with budget, status quo is fine. **Go to Hollywood.** |

Most ideas should die at day 90. That's the plan working.
